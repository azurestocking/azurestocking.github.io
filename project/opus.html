I. Script Editing

BEFORE

Relied on plain text with minimal markdown grammar, necessitating model inference for semantic understanding.

AFTER

Added a reformat option to declare key elements with symbolic tags for frontend styling and generative performance.

BEFORE

Used buttons with leading icons and labels for all options and a slider to adjust tone and length, causing multiple clicks.

AFTER

Simplified and grouped buttons with icons and tooltips, differentiated prompt input, and removed unnecessary two-step interactions.

II. Shot Editing

BEFORE

Misarranged controls with miscellaneous information architecture, juxtaposing hierarchical functions and nesting parallel ones.

AFTER

Reorganized controls by target objects and logical relationships across the floating toolbar and modal, and removed redundant buttons.

BEFORE

Opened a separate page with a disjointed layout where the triggering button fails to align with the output keyframe.

AFTER

Flattened the page hierarchy with modals and in-textarea select dropdowns to create intuitive cause-and-effect relationship.

BEFORE

Provided only voiceover for generating audio tracks, without distinguishing between spoken dialogue and narrative commentary.

AFTER

Separated dialogue from voiceover to support genres where audio diverges from visuals and voiceover alone is thus insufficient.

III. Transparency

AFTER

Added labels and tooltips to clarify technical terms and interface elements, reducing onboarding friction in a complex workflow.

AFTER

Clarified the AI’s capabilities, limitations, and decision logic, including which factors influence outputs, to manage expectations and build trust.

AFTER

Introduced a slideshow mode that simulates the final output’s pacing and structure before committing, minimizing uncertainty and costly re-renderings.

AFTER

Maintained an iteration log to help users compare changes between generations and make more informed revisions.
